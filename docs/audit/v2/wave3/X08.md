## Summary

The content moderation pipeline has a solid structural foundation (pre-save moderation on create, AI + word-filter dual check, report/queue/review workflow, appeals). However, it suffers from **systematic coverage gaps on edit paths** (findings 3-7: posts, comments, threads, reels, messages all lack moderation on update) and **incomplete content-type handling** (findings 1-2, 8-10, 23-24: threads, reels, and videos are second-class citizens in resolve, restore, auto-hide, and audit trail logic).

The most operationally dangerous finding is **#9 (urgent auto-hide weaponization)**: a single user can instantly censor any post by reporting it as NUDITY/VIOLENCE/TERRORISM with zero verification. This needs immediate attention before launch.

The **edit-path gap** (findings 3-7) is the largest systemic issue: every content type allows editing after creation without re-running moderation, enabling bait-and-switch attacks on viral content.
